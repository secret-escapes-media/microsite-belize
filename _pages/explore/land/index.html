--- layout: guide id: land title: Land group: explore group-order: 2 intro: Head inland and discover thick, tropical jungles, resplendent waterfalls, river valleys and towering Maya ruins. Belize is an eco-wonder, a playground of outdoor pursuits in sublime surroundings. Discover how to pack your itinerary with inland adventures... sections: - title: Maya Ruins description: The Maya civilization are one of the most mysterious in the Americas, having built vast cities, mastered astronomy, art and literature, and then inexplicably disappeared. Now, wild jungles are interrupted by vast temple complexes, expertly carved with hieroglyphics that hint at the artistic and intellectual mastery of the Maya. Belize is often lauded as the heartland of this great civilization, with a vast collection of ancient sights to explore. Set in the thickest of rainforest, adventure deep into the wilds to discover these wonders of the mesoamerican world. pois: - title: Altun Ha description: Though it may not be the biggest, Altun Ha is an iconic Maya site, immortalised on Belizean bank notes and on the label of the classic Belizean beer, Belikin. This easily-accessible complex dates back to around 200 BC, with potentially much older roots. Two large plazas are bookended by pyramids and mounds. Only a small portion of the site has been restored, with plenty still hiding in the thick jungle. - title: Xunantunich description: Though easily accessible, you’ll still feel like an adventurer having to cross a small river by way of hand-cranked car-ferry. A vast, towering pyramid is beset with detailed carvings, and a climb to the top offers stunning panorama over the river valley. A centre next to the ticket office shows a range of objects dug up during the excavations. - title: Lamanai description: Head up river to reach this vast Maya site – one of the most impressive in the country. Set on the banks of the New River Lagoon, towering pyramids, ball courts, residential areas and stelae converge on this inspired jungle scene. It’s one of very few Maya cities that was still populated during the Spanish Conquest, suggesting its importance to the people in the area. - title: Wildlife description: Belize is blessed with an abundance of colourful creatures tucked away in the thick, interior jungle. Vast river valleys and wetlands offer up diversity that has encouraged rich pickings of wildlife to settle in this country. From jaguars to tapirs and macaws to toucans, Belize is a playground of biodiversity. Spot it in a network of dedicated wildlife preserves, eclectic zoos, or just go exploring with your binoculars in hand... pois: - title: See the jaguars description: Cockscomb Wildlife Sanctuary opened the world’s only dedicated jaguar preserve, sheltering these endangered, magnificent creatures. Spanning 150 acres of lush rainforest, prepare for stunning walks under serene green canopies, past striking Maya ruins. See monkeys and birds and, with luck on your side, the elusive jaguars that prowl the grounds. - title: Spot monkeys description: The Community Baboon Sanctuary is an impressive grassroots project that has protected the endangered black howler monkey community, affectionately called baboons. A range of tours will have you spotting everything from monkeys to crocodiles, with nature hikes, river canoe tours, and immersive night tours at your disposal. Alight at Creole villages before heading back into the thick jungle. - title: See it all! description: Belize Zoo is a feast for the senses. See all the animals Belize is famous for, from tapirs to pumas, gibnuts to coatimundi, and plenty more colourful characters. What sets this zoo apart is the laid-back nature of its enclosures. Animals from the surrounding jungle flit around, climbing in with the tapirs or the peccaries. It really is a wondrous place that evidently does all it can for its residents. - title: Adventurers alight description: With a landscape framed by thick, tropical jungles, wild, streaming rivers, Maya ruins and sublime coastline, you’d be forgiven for getting a bit Indiana Jones when exploring Belize. This country caters to intrepid adventurers, from exploring a myriad of cultures like Creole and Garifuna, to losing yourself in dense jungle or kayaking down wild rivers. Push yourself to the limit and Belize will reward you with a paradise like no other. All you need to do is head out into the unknown... pois: - title: Jungle Hiking description: Head to the Mayflower Bocawina National Park, for trails that traverse wild terrain past Maya sites, thick jungle foliage, and magnificent falls. Black howler monkeys accompany your hike to the towering Antelope Falls, with breathtaking views from the top. Head further, for a demanding hike to Bocawina Falls, where a swimming spot awaits to cool you off. - title: Kayak the Nohoch Che’en Caves description: This impressive cave network makes for unforgettable adventuring. Head out on the quiet waters with a kayak or tube, drifting through as the cave envelops you. An unmatched tranquility awaits, as you glide into impressive formations like the Crystal Cave. It’s popular for good reason. - title: Diverse Cultures description: Belize is awash with a colourful variety of cultural groups. Head to Belize Town to sample Creole fare, or explore the south coast for Garifuna delights. Hopkins is a great example, where a stop at the Lebeha Drumming Centre is a great way to sample west African rhythms. Toledo is awash with old Maya villages, giving insight into centuries-old traditions and customs. ---
