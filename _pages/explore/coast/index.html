--- layout: group-page id: coast title: Coast group-parent: explore group-order: 1 parent-intro: Belize is a veritable paradise, where rare and unique sights await just under the ocean intro: >

Head for the coast, where balmy waters lap white-sand shores. Rare and unique sights await just under the ocean, whereas on dry land a distinctively Caribbean saunter typifies the laid-back coastal living. From technicolour aquatic life to relaxed beach bars, the coast of Belize is a true paradise.

sections: - id: under-the-sea title: Under the Sea content: Belize’s coast shelters some of the most impressive eco-systems in the world. The sea hides the largest coral reef in the Western Hemisphere. Add technicolour marine life and you have all you need for breathtaking aquatic adventures. So sling on your snorkel, or don that wetsuit, and discover an underwater world that will leave you reeling for more. highlights: - id: belize-barrier-reef title: Belize Barrier Reef content: 560 miles of technicolor reefs stretch off the Belizean coast from Cancun to Honduras. This eco-treasure invites snorkelers and divers to its crystalline waters, offering rare glimpses at an enchanting aquatic world. Tropical fish, sea turtles and manatees will guide you around the stunning reef. - id: blue-hole title: Blue Hole content: This natural curiosity has lured divers to its depths for generations. Ever since Jacques Cousteau named it as one of the top 10 diving spots in the world, it’s been remarkably popular. This underwater sinkhole with its recognisable dark waters, reaches a depth of 410 ft making it the largest of its kind. It’s said the waters clear the deeper you go, revealing the limestone cave features that lie below. - id: shark-ray-alley title: Shark Ray Alley content: Swim with sharks! It may go against everything that mother taught you but the docile nurse sharks and impressive southern stingrays offer a rare treat just below the water’s surface. Snorkel over and watch these magnificent creatures glide through the sea. - id: ambergris-caye title: Ambergris Caye content: A quintessential tropical paradise, Ambergris Caye has long been a hotspot for island dreamers. This long, thin spit of land is a laid-back ode to idyllic island living, where most of the ‘roads’ are little more than dirt; encouraging slow, calm traffic of bicycles and golf carts. To the south of the island you’ll find the capital, San Pedro, a town luckily devoid of any high-rises. From there, you’ll find a host of watersports to try, plus boat trips out to the reef for unmissable snorkelling and diving. highlights: - id: san-pedro title: San Pedro content: The capital of the island, San Pedro is a collection of colourful Caribbean buildings catering to tourists and locals alike. It’s a great spot for booking excursions to the reef, for diving adventures or adrenaline-fuelled watersports. San Pedro is blessed with a great restaurant scene perfect for sampling Belizean dishes al fresco. - id: hol-chan-marine-reserve title: Hol Chan Marine Reserve content: A section of reef in the southern part of the island is a dedicated marine reserve bursting with aquatic life. A renowned snorkelling spot, the shallow stretch offers rare glimpses at an abundance of colourful, tropical fish and coral. In this stretch you’ll find Shark Ray Alley, offering close encounters with these surprisingly docile creatures. - id: foodie-delights title: Foodie delights content: Island life on Ambergris is all about indulging oneself. Start your day at Estel’s for the best breakfast on the island. Banana pancakes and great views make for a great start to your day. Choose from Palapa Bar in the north for swim up beers in clear waters, or indulge at the Belize Chocolate Company. Be sure to try ceviche at Lily’s or Hurricane’s, for true island flavours. - id: caye-caulker title: Caye Caulker content: Island delights await at Caye Caulker. A sublime tropical landscape set with enviable white-sand beaches, it’s smaller and a fraction quieter than Ambergris. A classic backpacker’s destination, prices are cheaper here, yet with much of the same sublime spots that Belize’s Cayes offer. Caulker is all about taking things slow; recline on stunning beaches, sun yourself silly, saunter into town and indulge in island feasts of seafood and sweet treats. highlights: - id: lobsterfest title: Lobsterfest content: Caye Caulker comes alive in July, with the annual Lobsterfest offering all things crustacean. From lobster ceviche to lobster ice cream, delight in the irresistible ocean offerings with plenty of music, games and spirited events taking place across the island. - id: the-split title: The Split content: Hurricanes split Caye Caulker in half, leaving a northern, mangrove-filled nature reserve separated from the island. Rent a kayak and explore this tropical wonderland, with a host of colourful birds to spot as you slink down quiet waterways. Head back to the Split for chilled out bars with stunning views. - id: swim-with-manatees title: Swim with Manatees content: These loveable sea cows, irresistibly ugly and stupendously charming, are a precious sight; moreso, that they are endangered. Choose from a host of operators that run boat trips to catch a glimpse these marvellous creatures, but consider also which operator does the most conservation work. Once there, don your snorkel and spy a rare sight. ---
